USS Hudson-B

Summary

Location:: The Shackleton Expanse
Portrait::
Note:: The NCC-7832-B: A exploration and diplomatic exchange ship set to explore the Shackleton Expanse.

Description

A Federation Galaxy Class starship commanded by Edward Scriba.

Traits

  • Federation Starship
  • Galaxy Class
  • Survivor of Dominion War

Mission

The USS Hudson-B has been assigned to explore the Shackleton Expanse on the far side of the Klingon and Romulan empires. The Galaxy Class starship has been refit for service as an exploratory vessel after previously serving in the Dominion war. During the refit of the Hudson-B, the previously assumed lost USS Hudson-A reemerged from the Shackleton. Many members of the USS Hudson-A have been reassigned to the Hudson-B. The USS Hudson will be based out of Narendra Station, a deep space research station jointly run by Starfleet and the Klingon Empire.

Season 1

    Stardate 5428.8, Session: 1
    The USS Hudson-B arrived Bacchus-3, falling victim to a total power failure tripped by a massive subspace distortion. The subspace waves prevent long range communication and warp travel. They were found to be originated on Bacchus-3. An away team was put together to investigate the planet and hopefully deactivate whatever is causing the waves. On the planet, several Federation members were found, studying massive monoliths reaching into the upper atmosphere. The Hudson's away team was able to open a door on the monolith, revealing passageways inside.
  • Hudson Log 1.2 - Bacchus' Irresistible Call:
    Stardate 5428.8, Session: 2
    The Hudson's away team managed to deactivate the subspace distortion wave using the control room inside one of Bacchus' spires. Meanwhile, a Klingon Bird of Prey, The IKR Ya'Leth, fell out of warp completely deactivated much like the Hudson before.

    Deactivating the subspace wave triggers a security response, and the machinery in the planet began to rip apart the other planets in the solar system. The Hudson's crew assisted the Ya'Leth to eject their warp core, now resonating with the same subspace distortions as the planet.

    After escaping the Bacchus system, astronomers watched in horror and amazement as the entire Bacchus-3 system pulled itself apart, pulling inwards towards a singularity, and disappeared.

    Stardate 5557.6, Session: 3

The crew tracked the artifact down to the SS Tardigrade, an Orion trade ship, and convinced them to hand over the artifact before their Romulan liaison arrived. Back on Narendra Station, Admiral Stoker took the artifact to study further.

    Stardate 5697.6, Session: 5

Stoker ordered the Hudson to head directly towards the Pasqual system, which had previously been unscanned. The away team, led by Commander Lodge and joined by Jolepo, Verid, Doctor Suvak, and Ensign Tak avoided contact with the locals. They got beamed off the planet after 24 hours with film photographs and recorded observations.

  • Hudson Log 8 - Court Martial:
    Stardate 5832, Session: 10
    Admiral Banna Stoker was put on trial for a court martial. Commander Robert H. Lodge lead the prosecution on the request of Captain Alder. Defense Attorney Samuel T Cogley.

    Ahead of the trial, Jolepo and Verid investigated some of Admiral Stoker's history on the station, in particular the discharge of her son, Avery, a science officer aboard Narendra Station. They discovered that Admiral Stoker had suspected a Starfleet coverup regarding the Tilikaal and used her position to conduct a private investigation into the Subspace frequencies in the Washboard and their origin.

    Lodge argued through witness testimony that the Admiral had acted recklessly with her authority, and her removal from command was necessary to avoid prime directive violation and potential flashpoints leading to war with the Klingons. Cogley argued that the Admiral had acted within her rights in the chain of command and according to Starfleet values to provide assistance to a people in need.

    Finally, when Lodge offered into evidence the fact that Stoker may have been telepathically manipulated through the Tilikaal artifact, the tribunal ruled to acquit Stoker of charges, but reassigned her to a position far away from the Shackleton Expanse where her condition can be monitored.

  • Hudson Log 10.1 - Abyss Station:
    Stardate 5899.2, Session: 12
    Attempting to rescue Sukav, the Hudson chased a Romulan bird of prey to a black hole with a neutronium lined space station and a singular rocky planet orbiting it. The planet had strange cultist gazing at the black hole waiting for the day it would "gaze back". The black hole was rotating to annihilate the planet with its radiation jets. On the strange alien station, an away team tried to rescue Sukav.
    After a week of watching Sukav's tracking device linger in Romulan space, he was suddenly moved past Narendra station and into the Shackleton Expanse. The Hudson gave chase, pushing it's warp nacelles to ride over the washboard effect and catch up to the Romulan bird of prey.

    They found the bird of prey at a system orbiting a black hole, dubbed the Omega Draconis system. There, they found single rocky planet with no atmosphere but a series of underground structures about approximately 300 life signs. There was also a large space station made of obsidian black neutronium. The Romulans had taken Sukav, Lt Pruitt, and two Starfleet ensign s into the alien space station.

    As the Hudson arrived, the black hole suddenly seemed to rotate. Massive high powered radiation jets moved to expel along the galactic plane. The planet, and all ~300 people on it, stood to be annihilated. One away team went down to the planet to start an evacuation, while another went into the space station to recover Sukav and hopefully find out what caused the block hole to change orientation.

    The team on the planet found a small group of pale humanoids with space travel capabilities but no warp capability. They spoke about how they had spent a very long gazing into the abyss waiting for the time when the abyss would gaze back at them. They seemed unconcerned about the radiation jets.

    Meanwhile, a rescue team infiltrated the alien space station. Inside, they found large and humanoid-shaped automatons and a series of passageways leading into the center of the station. At the center, they found the Romulans working in a control center and the Starfleet officers tied up in the middle of the room. The rescue team ambushed the Romulans and engaged in the firefight. Kyle Miller and Tizia were each wounded by disruptor fire, leaving only Verid to fend off the remaining 5 Romulans.

    Verid and Sukav made the ultimate sacrifice to destroy Abyss Station, so that no one can use it again. On the planet, Nisha and Tak left the cultists on the service, unable to break them from their faith.

An away team found a single neutronium obelisk left on the planet, which seemed to have some sort of influence on the people there.

In just 110 years, they've reached the equivalent of earth's information age (already a time known for accelerated advances in technology.) After Jolepo expressed interest in visiting the planet, Captain Scriba agreed. Scriba, Jolepo, Jaro, and Delisle all went down, leaving Nisha and Tizia on the yacht.

    On the planet, the away team found a society with a singular focus: get into space. The people there had correctly deduced that the monoliths Starfleet removed we taken from them, and their entire culture had been driven to reach out to know what else was out there.

    The away team decided to find something like a library, and got directions to something the people there called repository. People there seemed to expect the away team to speak openly and constantly about what new projects and research they were working on. At the repository, the away team sought out information on the black neutronium monoliths that were removed from the planet over the last 100 years. What they found was a history of people quicky devising that someone from off world must have taken the obelisks, and that space-travel must be possible.

    In the repository, a weekly ideas-sharing session was held, where all in attendance were required to share their research and progress. In the middle of this atrium, a silo opened up, and one of the black monoliths rose up from below. Lieutenant Delisle felt a presence of malicious patience coming from within the silo. Jolepo scanned the area during the meeting and found a subspace signal with a some sort of sublimal messaging in it, similar to the artifact that influenced Admiral Banna Stoker 110 years ago.

    Jolepo presented on an energy-efficient waste-disposal system for a colony ship, which avoided any suspicion that they may be from off world. Once the meeting was over, the away team was able to find a place to be alone and beam off planet unnoticed.

    Before reporting back to Starfleet, the crew of the USS Portland also visited the second planet on their itinerary: Lilifas. Here, they did a survey on the pre-warp civilization below. Video footage revealed a species of centaur-like otter people with age-of-sail technology. While observing Lilifas, a tractor beam from the planet grabbed asteroid orbiting in a ring around the planet, and threw it towards the USS Portland. Lt. Nisha was able to dodge the rocks. The USS Portland then returned to Narendra station, with plans to recover the 1 monolith remaining on Pasqual-1.
